# Research Framework

## Business Problem

Healthcare providers must balance quality patient care with operational efficiency and cost management.

Understanding the drivers of healthcare utilization, treatment outcomes, and healthcare costs is essential for improving decision-making, resource allocation, and overall healthcare performance.

This project investigates patient admissions, medical conditions, insurance coverage, and billing patterns to identify factors influencing healthcare utilization and costs.

# Research Questions

## Patient Demographics

1. What is the age distribution of patients?
2. What is the gender distribution of patients?
3. Which blood types occur most frequently?

## Healthcare Utilization

1. Which admission types are most common?
2. Which medical conditions are most prevalent?
3. Which hospitals receive the highest patient volume?

## Financial Analysis

1. What is the average billing amount?
2. Which conditions generate the highest healthcare costs?
3. Which admission types generate the highest costs?

## Insurance Analysis

1. Which insurance providers are most represented?
2. What is the average billing amount by insurer?
3. Which insurers are associated with higher costs?

## Outcome Analysis

1. Which conditions are associated with abnormal test results?
2. Which patient groups appear most vulnerable?
3. Which admission types show poorer outcomes?

# Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if it:

* Produces meaningful healthcare insights.
* Identifies major healthcare cost drivers.
* Demonstrates strong data cleaning and analytical techniques.
* Includes SQL-based analysis.
* Includes a Power BI dashboard.
* Generates actionable recommendations.
* Showcases business and healthcare domain understanding.
* Demonstrates professional analytics workflow and documentation.
